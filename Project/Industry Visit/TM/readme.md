## 🧠 Industry Talk Reflection: Telekom Malaysia

### Connecting Data and Business

I had the opportunity to attend an industry talk by **Mr. Zaid Waqi Zulkifli from Telekom Malaysia (TM)**, where I gained valuable insights into how data is managed and applied in large enterprise environments.

The session helped me understand that data is not only a technical asset, but also a strategic resource that supports business operations, decision-making, and organizational growth. In large companies, data often comes from multiple systems and departments, so managing it properly requires structure, governance, integration, and a clear understanding of business needs.

One key takeaway for me was the importance of aligning technical solutions with business requirements. Tools and technologies are important, but they only create value when they solve the right problems and support organizational goals.

As a **Data Engineering student**, this talk broadened my perspective on enterprise data management. It reminded me that future data professionals must not only understand databases, pipelines, and analytics, but also how data connects to business processes and real decision-making.

Overall, the Telekom Malaysia industry talk was a meaningful session that strengthened my interest in **enterprise data systems, data engineering, and business-driven technology solutions**.

---

### 💡 Key Takeaways

* Data is both a technical asset and a strategic business resource.
* Enterprise data environments require strong integration, governance, and management.
* Technical solutions must align with business requirements.
* Data engineering supports decision-making across business functions.
* A strong data professional needs both technical knowledge and business understanding.
