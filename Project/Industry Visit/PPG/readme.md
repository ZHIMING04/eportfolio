## 🧠 Industry Visit Reflection: PPG

### Gaining Insights into Data Architecture at PPG

I had the opportunity to visit **PPG Coatings (M) Sdn Bhd** in Kuala Lumpur for an industry visit, where I gained valuable exposure to real-world data architecture and enterprise data practices.

The session helped me understand how modern organizations manage large volumes of operational data using structured data pipelines and integrated platforms. One of the most meaningful parts was learning how tools such as **SAP** and **Databricks** are used to support data flow, processing, reporting, and decision-making in an enterprise environment.

This visit gave me a clearer picture of how academic concepts such as **data pipelines, data integration, cloud platforms, and business intelligence** are applied in real industry settings. It also showed me that building a reliable data system is not only about technical implementation, but also about understanding business processes, data quality, scalability, and user needs.

A memorable takeaway from the visit was hearing from a senior who had completed an internship at PPG and successfully transitioned into a permanent role. Their experience gave me a better understanding of what companies expect from interns and how important it is to keep learning, take ownership, and contribute meaningfully during an internship.

Overall, the PPG industry visit was a valuable learning experience that strengthened my interest in **Data Engineering, Enterprise Systems, Cloud Data Platforms, and scalable data architecture**.

---

### 💡 Key Takeaways

* Enterprise data systems require strong architecture, not just isolated tools.
* SAP and Databricks can support large-scale data flow, processing, and reporting.
* Data pipelines must be reliable, scalable, and aligned with business operations.
* Real-world data engineering involves both technical skills and business understanding.
* Internship experience can become a strong pathway into long-term career opportunities.
